Escalation Levels

Level Type Action Escalate To
Level 1 Low Level Welfare Concern Record concern using the safeguarding portal. Monitor and gather information. Safeguarding Team Member
Level 2 Safeguarding Concern Record concern and notify safeguarding leadership. Review available information. Deputy Safeguarding Lead
Level 3 Serious Safeguarding Concern Immediate review and safeguarding intervention. Consider external agency involvement. Safeguarding Lead
Level 4 Immediate Risk of Harm Call emergency services immediately. Preserve evidence and ensure safety. 999 then Safeguarding Lead
Level 5 Missing Child Activate missing child procedure immediately. Coordinate search and reunification process. Safeguarding Lead and Event Management

Immediate Danger

If any child, young person or vulnerable person is believed to be in immediate danger:

  1. Call 999 immediately.
  2. Ensure the immediate safety of those involved.
  3. Notify the Safeguarding Lead.
  4. Record the incident through the safeguarding portal as soon as possible.

Missing Child Procedure

  1. Receive the report and obtain essential details.
  2. Notify the Safeguarding Lead or Deputy immediately.
  3. Check last known location and recent movements.
  4. Coordinate venue search activity.
  5. Escalate to police where appropriate.
  6. Record actions and outcome.
  7. Complete reunification record.

Record Keeping

All safeguarding concerns, incident reports and missing child alerts must be recorded using the NDTC 2026 safeguarding portal.

Records should include actions taken, decisions made, responsible personnel and final outcome.

Confidentiality

Information should only be shared with those who need to know in order to safeguard participants or manage the incident.

Safeguarding information must not be shared through unofficial channels or social media.

Review

This procedure should be reviewed before the event and updated following any significant safeguarding incident or organisational change.
