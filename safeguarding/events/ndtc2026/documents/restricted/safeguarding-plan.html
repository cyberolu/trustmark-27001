Event Safeguarding Plan

Internal safeguarding arrangements for NDTC Multi Sports Exhibition Event 2026.

Restricted Document

This plan is for authorised safeguarding personnel and event management only.

1. Purpose

This Event Safeguarding Plan sets out the safeguarding arrangements for NDTC 2026. It supports the safe management of children, young people, volunteers, officials, parents, spectators and event personnel.

3. Safeguarding Team

The safeguarding team will support the reporting, recording, escalation and management of safeguarding concerns, incidents and missing child alerts during the event.

4. Reporting Routes

  • Safeguarding concerns should be reported through the safeguarding portal.
  • Incidents should be recorded through the incident reporting form.
  • Missing child alerts should be reported immediately using the missing child form.
  • Emergency situations must be reported to emergency services by calling 999.

5. Missing Child Response

Any missing child report must be treated as urgent. The safeguarding team will coordinate initial information gathering, search support, escalation and reunification recording.

6. Confidentiality

Safeguarding information must only be shared with those who need to know. Sensitive records must not be shared outside authorised safeguarding or event management channels.

7. Record Keeping

All concerns, incidents and missing child alerts must be recorded in the TrustMark 27001 safeguarding platform. Case closure must include actions taken, outcome and responsible person.

8. Escalation

Serious safeguarding concerns, immediate danger, allegations of abuse or missing child incidents must be escalated to the Event Safeguarding Lead and, where required, to emergency services or statutory agencies.

9. Event Review

After the event, safeguarding records should be reviewed to identify lessons learned, unresolved issues and improvements for future events.
